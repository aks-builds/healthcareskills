# IRB Pathway Decisions

Reference for choosing among the three principal IRB review pathways — exempt, expedited, and full board — under the Common Rule (45 CFR 46) and the parallel FDA regulations (21 CFR 56). **Always verify current regulatory text and your institution's IRB policies before making a determination.** The 2018 Common Rule revisions changed several exempt and expedited categories; institutional policies may apply additional constraints beyond the federal floor.

## Regulatory framing

US human subjects research is governed by:

- **45 CFR 46 (the Common Rule)** — applies to federally supported research with human subjects. Subpart A is the main body; Subparts B (pregnant women, fetuses, neonates), C (prisoners), and D (children) add protections.
- **21 CFR 50, 56, 312, 812** — FDA regulations for FDA-regulated research (drugs, biologics, devices). FDA does not have "exempt" research the way the Common Rule does — FDA-regulated studies generally require IRB review.
- **OHRP** administers the Common Rule for HHS-funded research.
- **FDA** administers the FDA regulations.
- **Institutional policies** typically apply on top of the federal floor.

A study may be governed by the Common Rule only, FDA regulations only, both, or neither. The applicable framework drives every downstream decision.

## Exempt review

**Concept**: Research that meets one of the specifically listed categories at 45 CFR 46.104 is exempt from most Common Rule requirements but typically still requires an **exempt determination from the IRB** — not a self-declaration by the investigator.

**Categories** (per 45 CFR 46.104; verify current text — the 2018 revision modified several):

1. Research in established educational settings using normal educational practices.
2. Research using educational tests, surveys, interviews, or observation of public behavior, with certain limits on identifiability and risk.
3. Benign behavioral interventions with adults, subject to specific constraints.
4. Secondary research using identifiable private information or biospecimens, when conditions in 46.104(d)(4) are met.
5. Research and demonstration projects conducted by or subject to approval of federal agency heads, designed to study public benefit programs.
6. Taste and food quality evaluation studies.
7. Storage or maintenance of identifiable private information / biospecimens for secondary research, with broad consent (under specific conditions).
8. Secondary research using identifiable private information / biospecimens, when broad consent was obtained and IRB conducted limited review.

**Caveats**:
- **FDA-regulated research is generally not eligible for exempt determination** — verify against current FDA guidance and your institutional policy.
- **Subparts B/C/D constraints**: research with prisoners is generally not eligible for exempt determination; pediatric and pregnant-women research has further constraints.
- **Identifiability**: most exempt categories are conditional on de-identification, public-figure subjects, or specific protections.
- Some institutions apply a more restrictive interpretation than the federal floor.

## Expedited review

**Concept**: Research that is no more than minimal risk and falls within one of the listed **expedited review categories** can be reviewed by the IRB chair or one or more designated reviewers, rather than at a convened meeting of the full IRB.

**Categories** (OHRP / FDA list, last updated 1998; verify current text):

1. Clinical studies of drugs and medical devices where (a) an IND is not required and (b) the device is cleared/approved for marketed use and being used per labeling, or an IDE is not required.
2. Collection of blood by finger stick, heel stick, ear stick, or venipuncture from healthy adults, with volume limits.
3. Prospective collection of biological specimens by noninvasive means.
4. Collection of data through noninvasive procedures.
5. Research involving materials (data, documents, records, or specimens) that have been or will be collected solely for nonresearch purposes.
6. Collection of data from voice, video, digital, or image recordings made for research purposes.
7. Research on individual or group characteristics or behavior, or research employing survey, interview, oral history, focus group, program evaluation, human factors evaluation, or quality assurance methodologies.
8. Continuing review of research previously approved by the convened IRB (under specific conditions).
9. Continuing review of research that is not conducted under an investigational new drug application or investigational device exemption (under specific conditions).

**Minimal risk** (per 45 CFR 46.102) means the probability and magnitude of harm or discomfort anticipated in the research are not greater than those ordinarily encountered in daily life or during the performance of routine physical or psychological examinations or tests.

**Key constraints**:
- Expedited review **does not skip IRB review** — it is reviewed, just not by the full convened board.
- Expedited reviewers can approve, request modifications, or refer to the full board, but **cannot disapprove** — disapproval requires full board review.
- Subparts B/C/D may add constraints; verify before relying on expedited.

## Full board review

**Concept**: All research that is not eligible for exempt determination or expedited review must be reviewed at a convened meeting of the IRB with quorum, including the required diversity of membership (45 CFR 46.107, 21 CFR 56.107).

**Required when**:
- Research is greater than minimal risk.
- Research does not fit any exempt or expedited category.
- IRB judgment determines convened review is appropriate (e.g., complex risk profile, vulnerable population).
- Specific subpart triggers (e.g., research with prisoners under Subpart C generally requires full board with prisoner representative).

**IRB membership requirements** (per 45 CFR 46.107):
- At least five members with varying backgrounds.
- At least one scientist and at least one non-scientist.
- At least one member not otherwise affiliated with the institution.
- Knowledge of community attitudes and applicable law.
- Appropriate expertise for the research under review (consultants may be invited).

**Continuing review** of full-board-approved research:
- 2018 Common Rule revisions removed mandatory annual continuing review for many minimal-risk and certain post-data-collection studies; verify against current institutional policy and Common Rule text.
- FDA-regulated research has separate continuing review requirements (21 CFR 56).

## Special populations and Subparts B/C/D

| Subpart | Population | Key constraints |
|---------|-----------|------------------|
| B | Pregnant women, human fetuses, neonates | Additional risk/benefit and consent requirements; verify 45 CFR 46.204-46.207 |
| C | Prisoners | Limited categories of permissible research; prisoner representative required on IRB; verify 45 CFR 46.301-46.306 |
| D | Children | Permissible research categories based on risk/benefit (46.404 minimal risk; 46.405 minor increase over minimal risk with prospect of direct benefit; 46.406 minor increase without direct benefit; 46.407 referral to OHRP); parental permission and child assent considerations |

Always verify which subparts apply and whether the proposed research falls within permitted categories before proceeding.

## Single IRB (sIRB) reliance

The 2018 Common Rule revisions and NIH policy require a **single IRB of record** for most multi-site federally funded research conducted in the US, with narrow exceptions.

- Implemented via **reliance agreements** — local IRBs cede review to the sIRB.
- The **SMART IRB master agreement** is widely used among US academic institutions.
- Local context review (state law, institutional policies, language/literacy, recruitment considerations) still happens at each site, but full board review happens once at the sIRB.
- Industry-sponsored multi-site trials often use a **central commercial IRB** (e.g., Advarra, WCG IRB) per the sponsor's protocol.

Reliance setup takes weeks even when both sides are willing — plan accordingly.

## Pathway decision flow

```
Is the activity research with human subjects (per 45 CFR 46.102)?
   ├─ No  → not human subjects research; document determination
   └─ Yes
       │
       ▼
Is the research FDA-regulated (drug, biologic, device IND/IDE; FDA-jurisdiction)?
   ├─ Yes → exempt generally not available; expedited or full board per risk
   └─ No
       │
       ▼
Does the research fit a 45 CFR 46.104 exempt category AND meet category conditions
(de-identification, etc.) AND not involve prisoners (and other Subpart constraints)?
   ├─ Yes → seek exempt determination from IRB
   └─ No
       │
       ▼
Is the research no more than minimal risk AND in a listed expedited category?
   ├─ Yes → seek expedited review
   └─ No
       │
       ▼
Full board review

(plus: Subpart B/C/D constraints, single IRB requirement, institutional policies overlay throughout)
```

## Common misconceptions

- **"Exempt means no IRB involvement."** Most institutions require an IRB determination of exemption — the investigator does not self-declare.
- **"Expedited means the IRB does less work."** It just means review by chair or designated reviewer rather than convened board; rigor is the same.
- **"FDA-regulated studies can be exempt."** Generally not — verify against FDA guidance.
- **"sIRB removes site IRB involvement."** Local context review still happens; only convened review consolidates.
- **"Minimal-risk continuing review is required annually."** Post-2018 Common Rule, many minimal-risk studies no longer require annual continuing review — but FDA regulations and institutional policy may still impose it.

## Verification expectations

Before any pathway determination:

1. Verify current text of 45 CFR 46 (especially 46.102, 46.104, 46.110, Subparts B/C/D) at the eCFR.
2. Verify current text of 21 CFR 50 and 21 CFR 56 if FDA-regulated.
3. Verify the OHRP expedited review categories list (1998, with any subsequent updates).
4. Verify your institution's IRB policies — many apply constraints beyond the federal floor.
5. Verify NIH and any other federal funder policies on sIRB for multi-site research.
6. Verify state law where state-level human subjects protections apply.
7. Verify GDPR / EU CTR 536/2014 for EU subjects.

Always pull from the eCFR and current OHRP / FDA guidance — regulations evolve and institutional policy varies.
